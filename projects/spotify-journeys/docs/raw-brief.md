[Background
Spotify entered the Indian market in 2019 and has grown to over 100 million monthly active users. It dominates urban music streaming, competing with JioSaavn, Gaana, Wynk, and YouTube Music.

Spotify’s India strategy has relied heavily on:

Affordable premium plans (₹119/month individual, ₹59 student plan).

Localized playlists and vernacular content.

Aggressive partnerships (e.g., with Flipkart, telecom bundles).

But the problem: user engagement is shallow. While people stream music daily, the experience is passive. Retention is high, but differentiation is low — switching costs are minimal in music streaming.

Globally, Spotify has experimented with podcasts, audiobooks, and AI DJs to deepen engagement. But in India, leadership sees an opportunity to go further: position Spotify not just as an entertainment app, but also as a personal growth tool.

The Opportunity
Spotify’s India team has identified a user insight: many young professionals (18–30) want to use audio to learn skills, improve English, or build habits — but they currently turn to YouTube or niche apps (like Duolingo).

User research highlights this demand:

“I listen to English songs to improve my vocabulary, but there’s no structure.”

“I want audio lessons in bite-sized formats, like podcasts but more interactive.”

“Music motivates me, but I want it tied to goals like focus or language.”

Spotify believes the answer lies in Goal-Based Audio Journeys — turning playlists and podcasts into structured, skill-building tracks with nudges and progress tracking.

The Challenge
The India leadership team has asked the Strategic Initiatives team at Spotify, to ship an MVP of a Skill-Building feature in 3 weeks.

Constraints:

Engineering is focused on global podcast infra rollout → no dev bandwidth in India for 2 months.

The team has a product manager + designer +  business analyst available.

MVP should be built using AI product builders like Lovable, no-code backends (Airtable/Zapier/Supabase), and AI summarization tools.

Pilot audience: 1,000 student and early-career users in Delhi, Bangalore, and Mumbai from the existing Spotify base.

CEO’s directive:

“If Spotify can help people learn with audio, not just listen, we’ll have a unique edge in India. Prove this by shipping something simple but compelling.”

The MVP Vision
At its simplest, the MVP should:

Allow users to set a learning goal (e.g., “Improve English vocabulary,” “Practice public speaking,” “Focus better while working”).

Provide a structured audio track/playlist (e.g., 5 songs + 1 podcast clip daily).

Offer a progress tracker (days streak, completion %).

Deliver light nudges (reminders, motivational messages).

Other feature ideas that the team has:

Interactive quizzes.

AI-personalized learning paths.

Certificate/badge issuance.

Creator marketplace for “audio courses.”

Case Questions
What core features should be in the MVP to deliver value as a “skill-building” tool? What can be deferred?

Build a MVP prototype using Lovable/Replit/Bolt etc as a tool.

What success metrics would validate that users see Spotify as more than just entertainment?]
